# Risk Appetite Statement Template  
**XXXXX**

Defines acceptable levels of risk the organisation is willing to tolerate to achieve objectives.

---

# 1. Introduction
Risk appetite guides decision-making, resource allocation, control maturity, and escalation thresholds.

Applies across:

- operational risk  
- cybersecurity risk  
- financial crime  
- third-party risk  
- data governance  
- regulatory compliance  

---

# 2. Risk Appetite Levels

### **Zero / No Appetite**
- Fraud  
- Bribery & corruption  
- Data breaches of sensitive information  
- Non-compliance with laws and regulations  

### **Low Appetite**
- Operational downtime  
- Third-party outages  
- System failures  

### **Moderate Appetite**
- Innovation-related risk  
- Technology change risk  
- Controlled levels of operational error  

### **High Appetite**
- Strategic investment risk  
- Growth-related decisions  
- Market expansion initiatives  

---

# 3. Key Components

### 3.1 Qualitative Statements
Narrative statements describing attitudes toward each risk category.

Example:
> The organisation maintains low tolerance for cybersecurity risk and expects strong preventative and detective controls.

### 3.2 Quantitative Indicators
Metrics tied to appetite thresholds:

- % system uptime  
- maximum acceptable breach impact  
- number of failed phishing tests  
- security incidents per quarter  
- vendor SLA breaches  
- staff attrition metrics  

### 3.3 Early Warning Indicators (EWIs)
Indicators approaching risk appetite limit.

### 3.4 Hard Limits
Absolute boundaries that must not be breached.

---

# 4. Governance

- Reviewed annually or after major incident  
- Approved by Executive Committee  
- Monitored by Risk Committee  
- Changes require formal sign-off
