# Third-Party Contract Security Clauses

### Objective
Ensure vendors, partners, and service providers maintain adequate security controls, minimise liability exposure, and meet regulatory obligations.

---

## Key Clauses to Include

### **1. Security Obligations**
Vendors must:
- Maintain strong authentication and access controls  
- Encrypt sensitive data  
- Follow secure SDLC practices  
- Perform regular security testing  

---

### **2. Breach Notification**
Vendors must:
- Notify within X hours of discovering a breach  
- Provide details of affected data/systems  
- Deliver remediation steps and timelines  

---

### **3. Audit Rights**
Organisation may:
- Request evidence of security controls  
- Conduct or request third-party assessments  
- Review penetration testing results  

---

### **4. Data Protection Compliance**
Vendor must comply with:
- Applicable privacy laws (GDPR equivalents)  
- Data-processing obligations  
- Storage, retention, and deletion obligations  

---

### **5. Liability & Indemnification**
Vendor is responsible for:
- Notification costs  
- Incident response expenses  
- Regulatory fines due to their negligence  
- Legal costs  
- Service credit penalties (if applicable)  

---

### **6. Log Forwarding & Monitoring**
Contracts must explicitly require:
- Complete SIEM integration  
- Alerts for defined events  
- Evidence for verification  

(This prevents incidents like: “Vendor failed to forward firewall logs → Security events not detected.”)
